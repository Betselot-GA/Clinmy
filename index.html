Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is the difference between a car wash and car detailing?

    A basic interior car clean and an interior car detail can differ significantly according to who is performing the tasks and how comprehensively or safely the cleaning is performed, necessity and price. A basic one may consist of interior contact cleaning to get rid of surface dirt and grime and can be completed by hand. Detailers will perform the same but this will employ stages - such as shampooing and snow foaming – to cut down on the chance of inflicting stains and swirl marks later during the main contact wash. A car detail will often include extra processes such as polishing and adding surface protection, along with comprehensive interior cleaning. Auto detailing seeks to return the vehicle to its ideal state or significantly improve it.

  • How often should I have my car cleaned?

    Ideally, you’d want to have your car cleaned once every week. Once-a-week car clean will help you keep the interior of your vehicle well preserved.

  • Is it safe to take a car to your car wash during winter?

    As long as your car’s clean job has been effectively done inside, it is safe to pass through your local car cleaner or detailer. Our door-to-door Car Interior Care service actually helps preserve your car’s for longer during all seasons.

  • I have a newer vehicle. Do I still need to be concerned with Routine Interior Clean up?

    Absolutely, Environment Canada estimates up to nine million tons of salt are scattered on Canadian roads each year. Municipalities are now spraying calcium and magnesium chloride solutions on the roads prior to and after a winter storm. These deicers are often mixed with Beet Juice for better adhesion to the road surface. The result of this winter cocktail is an extremely corrosive substance which clings to all the metal parts of your vehicle including the carpet, seat screws and door panels.

  • Why do you only do Interiors only?

    While exterior car washes are easily available at most gas stations, interior cleaning presents a bigger challenge for car owners. Cleaning the inside of a car typically involves removing and washing mats, vacuuming, dusting, and polishing all hard surfaces from front to back. This process can be especially difficult in extreme weather conditions and often has to be done at home, which is time-consuming and labor-intensive. Commercial vacuum machines, often found at gas stations, are cumbersome to use. They require coins or credit cards and often don't provide enough time to complete a thorough cleaning, leaving car owners frustrated. The process of moving hoses around can spread dust and dirt, making seats dirty in the process. Additionally, home cleaning methods, especially without the right chemicals, often fail to leave mats and surfaces truly clean, with winter salt posing a particular challenge that can damage carpets if not addressed promptly. Overall, interior car cleaning is a tedious and time-consuming task that many car owners struggle to manage effectively on their own.
